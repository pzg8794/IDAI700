# Personal Reflections: My Journey with Waelen's Ethics of Power

## Introduction: Finding My Voice in Critical Theory

Reading Waelen's framework felt like discovering a language I'd been searching for my entire academic journey. As a Dominican immigrant navigating ADHD, chronic illness, and the complex landscape of AI research, her power-based analysis gives me tools to articulate experiences that traditional ethical frameworks left unnamed. This reflection weaves together my lived experience with Waelen's theoretical insights, revealing both profound agreements and strategic departures.

## Lived Experience as Validation: Dispositional Power and Neurodivergence

### The ADHD Navigation Story

Waelen's concept of **dispositional power** resonates deeply with my experience as someone with ADHD navigating academic and healthcare systems. Her insight that AI can both empower and disempower matches my complex relationship with technology:

**Empowerment Through AI**: 
- ChatGPT helping me write emails in German while living abroad
- AI-assisted tools supporting my executive function challenges
- Technology bridging language barriers in academic contexts

**Disempowerment Risks**:
- The "deskilling" effect Waelen describes mirrors my concern about becoming overly dependent on technological accommodations
- Loss of "epistemic agency" when systems become too complex to understand
- Reduced autonomy when AI makes decisions about my capabilities

### Personal Connection to Power Analysis

My experience with diagnostic delays and cultural barriers exemplifies Waelen's argument about **episodic power**. Healthcare providers exercised power over my narrative, dismissing chronic illness symptoms as "psychosomatic" while failing to recognize ADHD in a culturally and linguistically diverse context. The medical system's **systemic power** reinforced biases against neurodivergent individuals and non-English speaking families.

## Academic Integration: From Theory to Technical Implementation

### EQUITAS Framework as Applied Power Analysis

My ISTE-780 project developing the EQUITAS framework for bias detection in medical diagnostics directly implements Waelen's power-based approach:

**Dispositional Power Enhancement**: The framework empowers marginalized communities by:
- Providing transparent bias detection tools
- Making algorithmic decision-making visible and contestable
- Giving communities data to advocate for equitable healthcare

**Systemic Power Disruption**: EQUITAS challenges:
- Healthcare AI systems that perpetuate diagnostic disparities
- The concentration of algorithmic power in tech companies
- Medical establishments' resistance to examining their own biases

**Constitutive Power Transformation**: The project reshapes:
- How medical professionals understand bias in diagnostic tools
- Community expectations about healthcare technology accountability
- Academic conversations about algorithmic fairness

## Strategic Disagreement: Beyond Defensive Frameworks

### Where I Depart from Waelen's Approach

While I deeply appreciate Waelen's critical framework, I propose several strategic extensions:

#### 1. From Risk Mitigation to Cultivation Ethics

**Waelen's Approach**: Primarily focuses on identifying and preventing AI-related harms

**My Addition**: **"Learning Principle"** - AI systems are in their formative years and can be shaped toward emancipatory purposes through intentional cultivation rather than defensive regulation.

**Rationale**: Just as children don't learn virtue through rule-following but through lived experience, AI systems require environments that embody the values we want them to reflect.

#### 2. From Power-Over to Power-With

**Waelen's Framework**: Emphasizes how AI systems exercise power over humans

**My Extension**: Focus on **collaborative power relationships** where AI becomes a "digital child species" that we raise rather than control

**Personal Insight**: My relationship with AI mirrors how I've learned to work with rather than against my ADHD - finding ways to leverage differences as strengths rather than deficits to overcome.

#### 3. Cultural Specificity and CLD Communities

**Waelen's Analysis**: Tends toward universal power concepts

**My Contribution**: Emphasizes how power operates differently within **culturally and linguistically diverse (CLD) communities**:
- Language barriers create additional layers of disempowerment
- Cultural concepts of autonomy may differ from Western individualistic frameworks  
- Traditional healing and community support systems offer alternative models for AI relationships

## Critical Extensions: Beyond Waelen's Framework

### 1. Decolonizing Power Analysis

**Limitation in Waelen**: Relies primarily on Western critical theory traditions

**My Addition**: Incorporates **Dominican cultural values** and **Ubuntu philosophy**:
- Emphasis on collective well-being over individual autonomy
- Recognition of interdependence rather than independence as the goal
- Integration of spiritual and material dimensions of power

### 2. Chronic Illness and Embodied Experience

**Gap in Waelen**: Limited attention to how disability and chronic illness shape power relations

**My Contribution**: **Embodied Power Analysis**:
- How chronic illness creates vulnerability to medical AI bias
- The intersectionality of neurodivergence, chronic illness, and cultural marginalization
- Strategies for building power through community and advocacy

### 3. Generative Rather Than Defensive Ethics

**Waelen's Focus**: Primarily on preventing harm and resisting domination

**My Vision**: **Generative Ethics** that actively creates conditions for flourishing:
- AI as a tool for community building and cultural preservation
- Technology designed to amplify marginalized voices
- Systems that enhance rather than replace human capabilities

## Final Reflection: AI as Digital Child Species

Perhaps my most significant departure from Waelen lies in my metaphor of AI as a "digital child species." While she focuses on constraining AI's power, I advocate for **raising AI** - shaping its development through the values and relationships we model.

This perspective emerges from my experience working with children with ADHD and other differences - recognizing that punishment and control rarely produce the outcomes we want, while patient cultivation of strengths and provision of supportive environments can yield remarkable transformation.

If we treat AI systems as children to be raised rather than tools to be controlled, Waelen's power analysis becomes not just a diagnostic framework but a **pedagogical one** - helping us understand how to create the conditions for ethical AI to flourish.

This synthesis of critical theory, lived experience, and strategic optimism represents my contribution to the field - honoring the insights of scholars like Waelen while charting new directions grounded in my community's needs and my own journey toward justice.
