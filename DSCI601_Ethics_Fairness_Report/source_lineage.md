# Source Lineage for the DSCI601 Ethics and Fairness Report

## Foundation source

This report is derived conceptually from:

- `../Research Paper Portfolio/IDAI700_Research_Paper.pdf`

That paper argued that AI systems serving marginalized communities should be designed with
accountability, community power, and enforceable equity rather than trust language alone.

## Concepts carried forward

- Trust is not only a question of performance; it depends on accountability.
- Fairness should not be treated as decorative language or a post-hoc audit.
- Systems that optimize aggregate performance can still reproduce structural harm for specific groups.
- Design choices determine whose signals are legible, whose harms are noticed, and whose outcomes matter.

## Concepts intentionally left behind

- autobiographical opening and lived-experience framing
- the full EQUITAS institutional-governance argument
- the extended healthcare-only focus
- the long counterargument and AI-reflection sections required by IDAI700

## Why the new draft is different

The DSCI601 assignment is much narrower:

- one page instead of a long ethics paper
- tied directly to the current project design
- explicitly asks how the selected issue changes implementation and reproducibility

Because of that, the new draft reframes the ethics paper into a short fairness-centered project memo.
